Biowearable Technology

Revolutionizing healthcare with real-time biomarker monitoring and personalized medicine.

01 // The Problem

Conventional methods for biomarker monitoring involve venous blood draws, finger-prick tests, and urine analysis, which have significant limitations.

1.Invasive & Painful: Frequent blood testing is impractical and uncomfortable.

2.Episodic Measurements: Biomarkers fluctuate dynamically; single-point measurements fail to capture trends.

3.Delayed Diagnosis: Many conditions (e.g., metabolic disorders, heart attack) progress silently before clinical symptoms emerge.

Interstitial Fluid (ISF) as an Alternative to Blood

Interstitial fluid (ISF), which bathes peripheral cells and closely mirrors blood chemistry, has emerged as a viable biofluid for real-time monitoring. Unlike blood, ISF can be continuously sampled using minimally invasive microneedles, offering:

  • Comparable biomarker concentrations to blood plasma
  • No damage to capillaries or nerves
  • Near-painless, prolonged sampling
